Know Your Rights: Labour Laws Every Worker in India Should Know

📅 Apr 14, 2025✍️ Saral Rojgar Team⏱ 7 min read
⚖️

Every worker in India — whether daily wage, contract, or permanent — has legal rights. Knowing these rights protects you from exploitation and ensures fair treatment.

1. Minimum Wage

Every state in India sets a minimum wage for different types of work. In Uttar Pradesh, the minimum daily wage for unskilled workers is currently around ₹400–₹500/day. Employers who pay below this are violating the law.

2. Working Hours

By law, no worker should work more than 8 hours a day or 48 hours a week without overtime pay. Overtime must be paid at double the regular rate.

3. Provident Fund (PF)

If you work at a company with 20+ employees, both you and your employer must contribute to PF. This is your savings for the future.

4. Right to Safe Working Conditions

Your employer is legally required to provide a safe workplace. This includes proper equipment, safety gear, and protection from hazardous conditions.

5. Right Against Discrimination

No employer can discriminate based on caste, religion, gender, or region. If you face discrimination, you can file a complaint with the labour department.

6. How to File a Complaint

If your rights are violated, contact your local Labour Commissioner's office or use the government's Shram Suvidha Portal online to file a complaint.
