Know Your Rights: Labour Laws Every Worker in India Should Know

📅 Apr 14, 2025✍️ Saral Rojgar Team⏱ 7 min read
⚖️

Every worker in India has legal rights. Knowing these rights protects you from exploitation and ensures fair treatment.

1. Minimum Wage

Every state sets a minimum wage for different types of work. In Uttar Pradesh, the minimum daily wage for unskilled workers is around ₹400–₹500/day. Employers who pay below this are violating the law.

2. Working Hours

No worker should work more than 8 hours a day or 48 hours a week without overtime pay. Overtime must be paid at double the regular rate.

3. Provident Fund (PF)

If you work at a company with 20+ employees, both you and your employer must contribute to PF. This is your savings for the future.

4. Right to Safe Working Conditions

Your employer is legally required to provide a safe workplace — proper equipment, safety gear, and protection from hazardous conditions.

5. Right Against Discrimination

No employer can discriminate based on caste, religion, gender, or region. If you face discrimination, file a complaint with the labour department.

6. How to File a Complaint

Contact your local Labour Commissioner's office or use the government's Shram Suvidha Portal online to file a complaint.
